About Me

My Story

A California girl at heart, I was raised in the tri-valleys of the San Francisco Bay Area. As a kid I spent much of my time on various sports teams - soccer, gymnastics, horseback riding - and eventually focused on track & field. I attended UC San Diego where I received my B.A. in Psychology while running sprints on the school's track & field team. Next I married my high school sweetheart and we headed off to Utah where I received an M.S. in Marriage and Family Therapy. Following graduation, my husband and I spent 3 weeks exploring Northern Arizona and Southern Utah with friends before settling back in the Bay Area and starting our first big kid jobs. I spend a few days a week at the Standford University School of Medecine as a Clinical Research Coordinator on the Cancer Survivorship Research Team. I participate in the management and treatment of a phase III clinical trial seeking to improve sleep in women with breast cancer through talk therapy.

Beyond that, my interests are seemingly contradictory: I'm a homebody who loves to get outside and move. Favorites include trail running, yoga, cycling, mountain biking and canyoneering. I spend much time in my mental world, observing others, analyzing my own values, seeking consistent progression, and so my interests tend to lie in those things that help my mind to rest. Journaling in written, photo, and video form is one of those naturally mindful activities. I like to view it as a healthy outlet to my madness.

Did I mention My Husband?

He's my favorite human. My companion. My sanity. We met when we were just 14 and somehow the universe colluded to keep us together.
